The Mental Health Lifestyle

A Retrospective on Mental Health Trends in the United States

By Alice Zhu, Margaret Kato, and Phani Kuppa

According to the CDC, the United States is in a mental health crisis. The U.S. 2024 National Survey on Drug Use and Health revealed worrying statistics: more than 1 in 5 U.S. adults experience mental illness each year, and suicide is the 2nd leading cause of death among people ages 10-24. Was this always an issue? What factors are influencing mental health, and who is most affected?

First, let's survey the general population through the database of all human experience: Reddit.

Quarterly Reddit activity from 2011 to 2025 shows how selected mental-health communities grew alongside major general-interest subreddits, then breaks down which support communities drove that growth.

Small signals, big patterns: who is most affected by depression?

This analysis focuses on depression symptom burden using PHQ-based scoring methodology from the 2021-2023 CDC NHANES (National Health and Nutrition Examination Survey) dataset, providing quantitative insights into mental health patterns. Higher scores indicate higher depression severity, with 5, 10, 15, and 20 representing cutoffs for mild, moderate, moderately severe, and severe depression. Learn more about the NHANES here.

This analysis examines group-based differences in depression screening outcomes from the NHANES dataset. The study focuses on respondents who completed identical depression assessment items, investigating whether demographic patterns emerge when controlling for other variables. The research question addresses whether systematic differences exist between population groups despite similar screening protocols.

Weighted Analysis: Nationally representative mean PHQ-9 depression scores by gender, using NHANES MEC exam sample weights. PHQ-9 scale ranges from 0 (no symptoms) to 27 (severe). Population estimates derived from sum of survey weights.

As the demographic analysis shows, women on average have higher depression scores than men. Now, let's examine age-related patterns. How depression burden varies across different life stages within the same NHANES dataset?.

Younger age groups have higher PH1-9 depression scores.

Weighted Analysis: Nationally representative mean PHQ-9 depression scores by age group, using NHANES MEC exam sample weights. Age groups span 10-year intervals (18–29, 30–39, 40–49, 50–59, 60–69, 70+). Population estimates derived from sum of survey weights.

Age tells part of the story, but lifestyle factors may also play a role. The next analysis examines whether Body Mass Index (BMI) — a proxy for metabolic health — correlates with depression burden, and whether that relationship is linear or more complex.

Depression burden is highest in underweight populations.

Weighted Analysis: Nationally representative mean PHQ-9 depression scores by BMI category, using NHANES MEC exam sample weights. BMI categories follow standard clinical definitions in kg/m^2 (Underweight <18.5, Healthy 18.5–24.9, Overweight 25–29.9, Obesity ≥30).

BMI captures metabolic health, but another powerful lifestyle factor is sleep. The next analysis examines how weekday sleep duration relates to depression burden — and whether the data confirms the expected U-shaped curve.

Higher depression rates are correlated with abnormal sleeping habits.

Weighted Analysis: Nationally representative mean PHQ-9 depression scores by weekday sleep duration, using NHANES MEC exam sample weights. Extreme values (<2h or >14h) excluded as likely data errors.

As we can see, sleep patterns, BMI, age, and gender all show measurable relationships with depression burden. While more rigorous analysis is needed to decipher whether this is correlation or causation, it is clear that who is affected by mental health issues is related to both demographic and lifestyle factors.

The Mental Health Lifestyle: Final Thoughts

Using Reddit activity, national depression prevalence, and NHANES PHQ-based analysis, we explored the mental health crisis in the United States: as the mental health burden has become more visible and widespread over the past few decades, links to geographic location, lifestyle, and demographic factors have emerged. Although these visualizations cannot establish causation, they highlight clear patterns that can guide public health research and mental health intervention efforts in the future.
